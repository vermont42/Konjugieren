My grandfather Clifford Schmiesing was born in 1904 in Minster, Ohio, a town where German was the language of daily life. He grew up attending schools taught by German-speaking nuns, became a doctor, married Grace Ryan, and had six children. When WWII came, he served as an Army doctor in North Africa, delivering babies and treating wounded soldiers in Oran, Algeria. He died there on January 21, 1944. My mother was fourteen months old.

She never truly knew her father. But his love echoed through the generations. I feel it today.

Konjugieren is now available in the App Store. It is a German-verb conjugation app, and it is my tribute to Cliff and the language his ancestors spoke.

I have spent years building conjugation apps. First Conjugar for Spanish. Then Conjuguer for French. Konjugieren is the most ambitious of the three.

Here is what makes it special:

990 German verbs across every tense: present, past, perfect, subjunctive, imperative, future, and more. The app visually highlights ablaut changes, the vowel shifts that make German strong verbs so distinctive and so tricky to learn.

A 3,000-word essay traces the German verb system from the Proto-Indo-European steppe through Old High German to the modern language. You will learn why "sing, sang, sung" works the same way in English and German, and what ancient migrations have to do with it.

A timed quiz with three difficulty levels and Game Center leaderboards turns conjugation practice into a competition.

A wave-based minigame with German-themed enemies, power-ups, and sound effects makes using the app genuinely fun.

Deep iOS integration: Siri Shortcuts let you conjugate verbs by voice. Home Screen widgets surface a Verb of the Day. Live Activities track your quiz progress. VoiceOver support handles mixed German-and-English pronunciation gracefully.

Every tense includes a detailed article with literary examples from Goethe, Kafka, Mann, and Luther.

Cliff never got to see his grandchildren grow up. But the proud linguistic patrimony he embodied is alive in Konjugieren. I hope this app helps someone fall in love with the German language the way the Schmiesings once lived it.
