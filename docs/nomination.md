## Description (≤ 1,000 chars)

Konjugieren teaches German verb conjugation — created as a tribute to the developer's grandfather, Clifford Schmiesing (1904–1944), an Army doctor from German-speaking Minster, Ohio, who died serving in WWII Algeria.

This update brings two major additions.

A new Apple Intelligence tutor, powered by on-device Foundation Models, explains conjugation errors, drills weak forms, and adapts to the learner — fully private, free, no cloud calls.

A UI overhaul rebuilds verb, quiz, settings, and onboarding screens as elevated cards with semantic depth, embracing iOS 26's design language.

Accessibility: VoiceOver switches between German and English pronunciation per UI element — rare in bilingual apps. Ablaut vowel changes are announced semantically. AVSpeechSynthesizer provides German speech in quizzes.

Education: literary excerpts from Goethe, Kafka, and Mann; a 3,000-word essay traces verbs from Proto-Indo-European to modern German.

Bilingual EN/DE. iPad multitasking. Game Center.

## Helpful Details (≤ 500 chars)

Built solo by Josh Adams — also creator of Conjuguer (French) and Conjugar (Spanish). Konjugieren honors a family link to Minster, Ohio: a German-speaking town where his grandfather grew up before serving as an Army doctor in WWII.

Bilingual VoiceOver is hand-engineered: each UI element carries its own pronunciation locale, switching mid-sentence between German and English. Most bilingual apps pronounce everything in the device language. It is the platform craft Josh is proudest of.
